# Accessibility Direction[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#accessibility-direction)
## Overview[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#overview)
The Accessibility team makes the GitLab product and Pajamas Design System more accessible for our users. As a team within the [Foundations stage](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/), we work closely with the [Design System team](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/design_system/), and designers and frontend engineers across GitLab. We lead accessibility initiatives, improve our accessibility workflows, and train and mentor other GitLab team members in digital accessibility. Our work ensures GitLab is available to all users, supporting our mission to enable everyone to contribute to and co-create the software that powers our world.
## Mission[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#mission)
Our mission is to do our part to ensure the GitLab product lives up to it's mission, enabling everyone to contribute to and co-create the software that powers our world.
## 1-Year Plan[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#1-year-plan)
### Technical Implementation & Issue Resolution[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#technical-implementation--issue-resolution)
  * Work through existing backlog of identified accessibility issues
  * Implement accessibility solutions using semantic HTML, ARIA, CSS, and JavaScript, following WCAG guidelines
  * Conduct code reviews for accessibility compliance and provide actionable feedback
  * Identify new issues through automated and manual testing


### Process & Documentation[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#process--documentation)
  * Create and maintain accessibility documentation, including guidelines and best practices
  * Improve accessibility testing and development processes
  * Develop organized backlogs for systematic issue resolution
  * Maintain Accessibility Conformance Report (ACR) by evaluating the product against accessibility standards


### Training & Enablement[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#training--enablement)
  * Provide basic accessibility training and guidance to UX and development teams
  * Enable designers, engineers and PMs to deliver the most accessibily features possible


### Cross-Team Collaboration[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#cross-team-collaboration)
  * Work alongside the Design System team on shared accessibility goals
  * Partner with designers and frontend engineers across GitLab to deliver accessible features
  * Support existing product teams in prioritizing and resolving known accessibility issues


### Culture Building[](https://about.gitlab.com/direction/foundations/accessibility/#culture-building)
  * Create opportunities for hands-on learning through bug bashes and other initiatives
  * Host office hours, Q&A sessions and deep dives on specific accessibility challenges
  * Engage directly with users with disabilities to build empathy and understanding
  * Build a culture of advocacy throughout the organization
