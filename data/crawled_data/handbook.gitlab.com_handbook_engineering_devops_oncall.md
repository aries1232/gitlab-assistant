# Tier 2 On Call
Welcome to the DevOps Tier 2 On-Call program! This documentation will guide you through everything you need to know about being on-call for our platform.
## What is Tier 2 On-Call?[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/#what-is-tier-2-on-call)
[Tier 2 on-call](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/understanding-the-basics/) is the second level of incident response here at GitLab meant to engage Subject Matter Experts from the Core DevOps domains. When critical issues affect our services, Tier 2 engineers are the specialists who handle domain-specific problems that go beyond what our first-line responders can resolve. Tier 2 on-call integrates with other on-call rotations including IMOC (Incident Manager on Call) and EOC (Engineer On Call), which all follow similar [handoff continuity](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/handoff-and-continuity/), and [communication and culture](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/communication-and-culture/) guidelines. However, you should not be in [multiple rotations](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/coverage-and-scheduling/#multiple-rotations) if you are participating in Tier 2 on-call.
## Getting Started[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/#getting-started)
New to the program? Start [here](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/your-first-shift/) by learning what Tier 2 on-call means and how it fits into our incident response structure. We’ll guide you through everything you need to prepare for your first on-call rotation. When you’re actually on-call, you’ll need to know what to do when you’re paged and how escalation works. Our on-call platform, Incident.io, has specific technical setup requirements and usage guidelines. You’ll rely on runbooks and playbooks for documented procedures, and you’ll need to [understand how to properly hand off](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/handoff-and-continuity/) to the next engineer to maintain continuity.
## Program Management[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/#program-management)
Understanding how the rotation works is essential. Learn how team members are [added, removed, and tracked in the system](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/coverage-and-scheduling/). We distribute shifts fairly across the team, and you can easily view your schedule. We also have clear processes for managing [the schedule](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/rotation-leader/#the-schedule) to ensure adequate coverage, as well as [joining and leaving rotations](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/joining-and-leaving-rotation/).
## Tracking and Improvement[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/#tracking-and-improvement)
We track performance metrics to measure [success](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/measuring-success/) and understand what we’re aiming for, and Rotation Leaders perform [proactive reviews](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/rotation-leader/#regular-reviews). We actively seek feedback and suggestions for improvements, and we welcome you to share your experience with the program.
Questions? Ask your [rotation leader](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/devops/oncall/rotation-leader/). We’re here to help you succeed!
