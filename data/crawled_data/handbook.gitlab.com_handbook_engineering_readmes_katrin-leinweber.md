# README of Katrin Leinweber
Personal readme page for Katrin Leinweber, Senior Support Engineer, GitLab
## Katrin’s README[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/readmes/katrin-leinweber/#katrins-readme)
Hi, I am Katrin! Biochemist by training, but IT generalist by choice. Having used Git in its various forms since 2014, I’m keen diving deeper into the GitLab-surrounding technologies and applications.

## My working style[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/readmes/katrin-leinweber/#my-working-style)
I tend to jump in quickly upon recognizing a task I can complete, going into sleuthing-around-logs-and-code mode when necessary.
## Communicating with me[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/readmes/katrin-leinweber/#communicating-with-me)
I enjoy working asynchronously via issues and MRs. Particularly also with GitLab’s `To Do` function. Please use `@katrinleinweber` liberally.
For urgent, synchronous communication, I’m fine with Slack or Zoom, but please be sure it is really urgent.
E-mails are not a good choice, as I check them only rarely, some times not even once a week.
## Weaknesses[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/readmes/katrin-leinweber/#weaknesses)
  * Too little stepping-back-and-seeing-the-big picture, when working on urgent tasks.
  * Good ideas sometimes come to me only after a good night’s sleep.


## Strengths[](https://handbook.gitlab.com/handbook/engineering/readmes/katrin-leinweber/#strengths)
Good ideas come to me usually after a good night’s sleep, or a walk through the [great outdoors](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AzttaR1PIlM).
Because I worked in different industries and contexts, I think I can rely on a broad base of experiences and problem-solving approaches.
